The causes for the additional decay are the presence of microbes that decay chloramine and/or chlorine has been overdosed.
If it is not due to overdose, please analyse possible options using Scenario analysis.
Also note that nitrate may not appear immediately even if • sign is seen if heterotrophic bacteria are controlling.
If the reservoir status is • and if improved (e.g. by dosing chlorine), status will turn to • , but will take time for nitrate to come down.

Notes on Option Analysis

All improvement options assume inlet nitrite concentration is < 0.005 mg/L

If nitrification and decay statuses are • and temperature is >18 degrees C and increasing, monitor BRC (optionally nitrite) regularly or try and bring the status to yellow or green by intervention.
In all cases, chloramine should be consistently above BRC (i.e. • ).
 If nitrite is < 0.01 mg-N/L in the reservoir, multiple rechlorination may improve chlorine, but dosing of smaller doses should be avoided as this can negatively influence the chloramine stability.
If nitrite is < 0.04 mg-N/L in the reservoir, multiple rechloramination may improve chlorine.
For any nitrite level, multiple (daily) partial breakpoint chlorination may improve chlorine.
 If nitrification and decay statuses are • but temperature is < 18 degrees C and cooling, rechlorination may be sufficient.
